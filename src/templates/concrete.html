Our Concrete Process

Excavation & Base Prep

The first step in creating a beautiful concrete slab is to build the base. We dig down about 12" then add about 8" of compacted Granular "A" crushed stone.


Forming

After the base is in place we build the forms for the project. Setting the forms corectly is obviously very important because they determine the shape and grade of your concrete slab. Our forms are built from wood and nailed to either steel or wooden stakes to keep them in place. We coat the wooden forms with oil before pouring the concrete so that they come apart from the concrete easily. Just before pouring we double check our form placement and level making any required adjustments.


Pouring & Finishing

The concrete is delivered to the work site in a concrete truck. We prefer to pour with a 4.5" - 5" slump. The concrete is poured inside the forms and we rake it out to grade. Next we bring the concrete to a smooth finish while starting to add some dye powder for colour.


Stamping

With the surface and edges brought to a smooth finish and some colour added we now broadcast powder release dye all over the slab with a mason brush to ensure full and even coverage. We also dust the bottom of each stamp with powder release dye and when the concrete is the perfect firmness we start stamping. We start with a texture stamp all around the edges. Then begin from a corner and stamp our way over the entire slab tamping down the stamps and walking over them as we go.

We use the touch up wheel to fix joints where concrete got squished up between stamps. We also do detail work agaisnt the walls where stamps don't fit. Finally we broadcast more release powder over the slab.


Finishing Touches

After stamping we wait 48 - 72 hours and go back to add some finishing touches. First we sweep off excess release powder and thuroughly pressure wash the surface. Next we use a grinder with a diamond tippped blade to remove excess concrete from squishups we missed during stamping. For any joints that appear too faint we use chizzels to etch them out correctly. Now the repairs done to these spots will have revealed bare concrete so we mix up some colour and apply it to those areas.

If any small cracks have appeared we repair them by tapping them closed with a hammer. Finally we clean off the surface with a leaf blower and then spray on a couple coats of sealant. We will also wait a couple of weeks until the concrete is fully cured to come back and seal it again. In the best interests of keeping the concrete in top condition the home owner should inspect and maintain the sealant as required going forward .
