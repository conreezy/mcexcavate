Our Process

Excavation & Base Prep

The first step in creating a beautiful concrete slab is to build the base. We dig down about 12" then add about 8" of compacted Granular "A" crushed stone.


Forming

After the base is in place we build the forms for the project. Setting the forms corectly is obviously very important because they determine the shape and grade of your concrete slab. Our forms are built from wood and nailed to either steel or wooden stakes to keep them in place. We coat the wooden forms with oil before pouring the concrete so that they come apart from the concrete easily. Just before pouring we double check our form placement and level making any required adjustments.


Pouring & Finishing

The concrete is delivered to the work site in a concrete truck. We prefer to pour with a 4.5" - 5" slump. The concrete is poured inside the forms and we rake it out to grade. Next we bring the concrete to a smooth finish while starting to add some dye powder for colour.
