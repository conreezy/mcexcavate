Our Paving Process

Excavation

The first step to repaving a driveway is to remove the old apshalt. Often we rebuild the base of the driveway as well. When rebuilding a base we excavate down about 12".

Base Preperation

If we are only removing the old asphalt we will add some crushed stone to correct any grading isues in the current base and then compact the base with rollers and plate compactors". If we are re-doing the whole base we will add the appropriate amount of granualar 'A' stone (usually about ten inches), compact it and grade it properly to prepare for paving.

Paving

Once the base has been prepared it's time to put down the asphalt. For this we use a paver. The paver is filled with hot asphalt which is brought to the job site in one of our dump trucks. First the skid steer will drop buckets We use the paver to smoothly cover the entire surface with asphalt. Workers touch up and clean up the surface as we go along. Once the entire surface has been laid down we roll the asphalt.

After Care

Once your new asphalt driveway is in place, it will require care and maintenance, both while it’s new, and over its lifetime.

The following is a guide to help you care for your new asphalt over it's curing period of a few months. One of our crew leaders will explain the care procedures to you on installation day. You are also always welcome to call us anytime with questions and concerns.

First 14 Days

New asphalt is most vulnerable during the first 14 days after paving therefore it is critical to take proper care of your new driveway during this time.

  • Do not drive on the asphalt for 3 to 5 days after completion.
  • Do not park on the asphalt for 14 days.
  • It is ok to walk on the new asphalt.

Next 100 Days +

While the first couple of weeks are most important the new ashalpt driveway remains somewhat vulnerable while it cures over next 100 days and the care or lack thereof that your freshly paved driveway is provided can have a lasting effect on the quality of the surface.

  • Park in different spots for the first 12 months to avoid creating ruts in your new driveway.
  • While the asphalt is curing it will be more susceptible to becoming very soft during extreme heat. Water the new asphalt on hot days to harden it.
  • Keep excessive weight off the asphalt. If parking a camper or boat on the new surface place a piece of plywood under each tire and under the trailer tongue.
  • Lawn chairs, barbeques, bicycle and motorcycle kickstands exert weight on concentrated areas and will create holes and depressions in your new driveway, especially in hot weather.
  • Avoid driving on edges of the asphalt. These are the weakest points and will crack and crumble over time.
  • Avoid spilling gasoline, oil, anti-freeze, power steering fluid, transmission fluid etc. These will dilute the liquid asphalt in your blacktop. Any holes left by these spills should be filled with cold patch.
  • Any hairline cracks that may have developed over the winter due to the contraction and expansion of the ground should be filled with crack filler.
  • Apply a seal coating one year after the asphalt has been applied and then every 2-3 years thereafter. Unprotected driveways remain porous, dry out, become rough, and lose their life rapidly.

What is Asphalt?

"Asphalt cement, black or brown petroleum-like material that has a consistency varying from viscous liquid to glassy solid. It is obtained either as a residue from the distillation of petroleum or from natural deposits. Asphalt consists of compounds of hydrogen and carbon with minor proportions of nitrogen, sulfur, and oxygen." - britannica.com

Hot mix asphalt (HMA) is used to pave roads, parking lots and driveways it "consists of a combination of aggregate uniformly mixed and coated with asphalt cement. To dry the aggregates and to obtain sufficient fluidity of asphalt cement for proper mixing and workability, both the aggregate and asphalt must be heated prior to mixing—hence the term “hot mix.” Aggregates and asphalt are combined in a mixing facility in which all of the constituent materials are heated, proportioned and mixed to produce the desired paving mixture." - mcasphalt.com
