  • Serving Central Ottawa

We set our mowers to cut at 2.5" - 3" and won't cut off more than one-third of the grass blade. Cutting more than one third of the blade shocks the grass and deters its ability to support root growth because the roots shut down their growing process while the leaves and shoots of the plant are regrowing.


We recommend leaving the grass clippings on the lawn because as they decompose they add nutrients to the soil. We do offer as part of our service to pick up the clippings. If we do leave the clippings on the lawn we never leave a mess; any clumps of clippings will be broken apart and dispersed around the lawn using leaf blowers.


Because of the heat and slow grass growth in July and August we may cut the grass every two weeks. We will always stop by to check whether we should cut the lawn or not and clean things up with the trimmers and blowers. In October and November it is often too cold for regular grass growth so the same situation applies. Fairway has been mowing lawns in Ottawa since 2008.

Fertilizing

  • 4 applications of fertilizer.  
    #1 - Spring  
    #2 - Early Summer  
    #3 - Late Summer  
    #4 - Fall
  • Balanced fertilizer blend appropriate for the time of year.

In order for grass to be healthy in the long term the root system must constantly grow and grass must constantly reproduce supplying the system with young grass and deep roots. Over time soil loses it's nutrients. To supplement this natural loss in nutrients​ lawns should be fertilized to keep the grass well fed.


Fertilizers contain nitrogen, phosphate, and potassium. The percentage of each element in the blend depends on the time of year.

Aeration

Aeration is done using a gas powered machine called an aerator. The machine drives over the top of the lawn and removes plugs of soil and grass every 6" - 8" or so and leaves them on the lawn.


The holes are filled in by the surrounding soil which creates more air space in the lawn and root system. This increased air space allows water and nutrients to flow better, feeding the roots deeper and more effectively.


The plugs decompose and add nutrients to the soil.

Dethatching

Dethatching is basically a very effective and thorough raking of your lawn.


A gas powered machine called a dethatcher (a.k.a. power rake) is used to bring dead grass and other organic material found at the base of the roots to the surface so it can gathered up and removed.


This creates more room down by the roots so water, nutrients and sunlight can more easily flow.

Overseeding

As grass ages it slows and eventually stops reproducing on its own. Therefore to have a healthy and thick lawn in the long term overseeding should be done every season.

Slit Seeding

Slit seeding is an alternative to overseeding it is done using a slit seeding machine. The machine moves over the s​urface of the lawn, cuts a small slit in the ground and places grass seed there. This protects the seed from being blown away, and from being eaten by birds and other animals.


Top Dressing

Top dressing is adding a thin layer of soil or manure or organic material on top of the lawn. This is to add nutrients to the existing soil.


After we put down the organic material we rake it out using fan rakes to make sure it settles to the bottom of the grass.


We recommend top dressing if your lawn has lost its green colour or is sparse.
